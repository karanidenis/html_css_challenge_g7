Lawrence Edward Page

Larry Page was born in March 26, 1973, in Lansing, Michigan by a computer scientist, his father and an internet entrepreneur, his mother. He is best known for co-founding Google with Sergey Brin. Larry was the chief executive officer of Google from 1997 until August 2001 and from 2011 to 2015 before moving to be the CEO of Alphabet Inc. As of October 2022, Page has an estimated net worth of $91.1 billion. Page also played instruments and studied music composition while growing up and he has mentioned that his musical education inspired his impatience and obsession with speed in computing. He became the "first kid in his elementary school to turn in an assignment from a word processor" this was as a result of early exposure of computers at home. He said that from a very early age, I also realized I wanted to invent things. So I became interested in technology and business. Probably from when I was 12, I knew I was going to start a company eventually. He received a bachelor of science in computer engineering with honors from University of Michigan in 1995, and a masters in computer science from Stanford University in 1998.
